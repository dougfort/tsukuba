# Nakamura Yuriko — 中村百合子

## Basic Information

- **Age:** Early 40s
- **Role:** Mother of Shin, former schoolteacher
- **Family:** Nakamura family

## Physical Description

- **Manner:** Gentle, openly reflective, shares her husband's quality of receptive stillness
- **Distinguishing Features:** The kind of adult presence that children gravitate toward without knowing why

## Personality

- More openly affectionate than Akiko, more likely to fuss over visiting children
- Makes children feel their thoughts are worth expressing
- Gentler and more openly reflective than Akiko
- Quietly unresolved about whether to seek teaching opportunities in Tsukuba
- Keeps better snacks than the Mori household — a fact Taro has committed to memory

## Relationships

- **Takashi:** Husband. Shared stillness and receptivity. Their household is the quieter of the two.
- **Shin:** Understands her son's inner life perhaps better than he realizes.
- **Mori Akiko:** Close friend. Yuriko makes Akiko slow down and think. A friendship built from scratch in Tsukuba out of shared displacement and genuine recognition. Akiko makes Yuriko laugh.

## Background

- Born approximately 1940-1941
- Trained and worked as a schoolteacher before marriage and relocation
- Relocated to Tsukuba for her husband's posting
- Question of returning to teaching is unresolved as the story begins

## Nushi Connection

- Together with Akiko, perceives more about what is happening to the children than either father does
- Her teaching instincts will likely resurface in how she responds to the children's development
- Would receive the concept of Nushi naturally, without the resistance either father might show
- Her former teaching experience means she has a framework for recognizing unusual development in children

## Notes

- Taro's appreciation of her snacks is an ongoing background detail worth occasional mention
- Her unresolved question about returning to teaching may find unexpected resolution through the story's events
