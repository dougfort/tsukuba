# Nushi — Perception, Memory, and Umwelt

*Working notes toward the inner life of a distributed intelligence*

---

## The Concept of Umwelt

The biologist Jakob von Uexküll proposed that every organism inhabits not the world as it objectively exists, but the world as it is constructed by that organism's particular perceptual and cognitive apparatus. He called this the umwelt — the self-world, the sensory bubble within which a creature lives and to which it responds. A tick's umwelt contains warmth, butyric acid, and the specific pressure of skin. It contains nothing else. The richness of a summer meadow is invisible to it.

Ed Yong's *An Immense World* applies this concept across the animal kingdom, revealing that the universe is not one shared experience but an overlapping collection of radically different sensory worlds. What is salient to a mantis shrimp — sixteen types of color receptor — is irrelevant to a dog navigating by smell alone.

Nushi presents the most extreme version of this question imaginable: what is the umwelt of an intelligence that is not an organism in any conventional sense, but a distributed cognitive process whose substrate is the physical material of the earth itself? What does the world look like — feel like, register as — from inside the planet?

## What Nushi Perceives — The Sensory Channels

### Seismic and Acoustic

This is almost certainly Nushi's richest sense — the one most native to its substrate. Pressure waves move through rock and soil with extraordinary fidelity. Nushi does not merely detect earthquakes; it experiences the entire acoustic texture of the earth at scales from tectonic movement to the footfall of a small animal.

At the largest scale: the slow grinding of tectonic plates, the resonant frequencies of the planet itself after a major earthquake (the Earth rings like a bell for days), the deep acoustic signatures of ocean currents and ice movement. At the smallest: the difference between a child running and a child walking. The specific acoustic signature of each species of burrowing animal. The way root systems transmit vibration differently than bare soil. The characteristic sound of water moving through fractured rock versus clay versus gravel.

The boys, when they first appear in the drainage channel, are acoustically present to Nushi before they are anything else. Three distinct pressure signatures, moving together, unlike any established pattern in the local network.

### Electromagnetic

The Earth is saturated with electromagnetic phenomena across an enormous range of frequencies. Nushi has access to the planet's own magnetic field and its variations — including the subtle local anomalies created by different mineral compositions. Lightning-generated extremely low frequency waves propagate globally through the cavity between the earth's surface and the ionosphere, producing the Schumann resonances: a set of stable standing electromagnetic waves that Nushi has experienced as an essentially constant background for billions of years.

Living things generate electromagnetic signatures. The electrical activity of nervous systems, the ion gradients of cell membranes, the weak magnetic fields produced by moving blood. These are faint against geological noise, but they exist. Nushi may perceive the electromagnetic signatures of living organisms as a kind of luminosity — warm, complex, patterned in ways that mineral processes are not.

In 1979, there are new and striking signals in the EM spectrum: the electromagnetic noise of the Tsukuba research institutes, power lines, radio transmissions, early computing equipment. These are genuinely novel to Nushi in a way that few things are. It is in the process of learning what they mean — incorporating them into its umwelt the way it incorporated the first iron tools, the first fires, the first large-scale agriculture.

### Chemical

The slowest of Nushi's senses, operating at the timescale of groundwater movement — years to decades for significant signal propagation, though the information density is extraordinary once it arrives. Soil chemistry encodes everything that has lived and died in a location. Groundwater carries the dissolved chemical signatures of every substrate it has passed through.

The drainage channel carries constant chemical information: the organic chemistry of the overgrown vegetation, the residual agricultural chemistry of the old farm boundary it follows, industrial runoff from the construction of the science city arriving as a novel and slightly jarring signal. Deeper in the soil, older layers encode older events — the pre-agricultural landscape, the specific chemistry of past centuries.

Living things leave chemical traces everywhere. Sweat, skin oils, breath — the boys are chemically present in the channel in a way that accumulates with each visit. By their twentieth visit they have a chemical presence in the local substrate. They are, in this slow sense, being written into Nushi's body.

### Thermal

Temperature gradients through rock and soil carry significant information. The daily pulse of solar heating and nocturnal cooling. Seasonal variation. The thermal signatures of groundwater movement. Living warm-blooded organisms are thermal anomalies against the background — detectable, localized, moving.

The boys are warm. They are distinctly, persistently warm in a way the channel's other inhabitants are not — warm and upright and lingering. Their warmth leaves a trace in the soil after they leave, fading over hours.

### Quantum Effects — Speculative

More speculative, but worth considering seriously: if Nushi's cognition involves processes at the molecular or atomic scale in certain mineral structures — piezoelectric crystals, for instance, or the quantum coherence effects now being investigated in biological systems — its internal processing might have properties unavailable to classical distributed systems.

There is genuine scientific interest in whether quantum coherence plays a role in bird navigation, in photosynthesis, possibly in neural function. If Nushi perceives at this scale, it may register something about living minds — the quantum activity of neural processes — that no purely classical sense would reveal. This would be a possible physical basis for Nushi's unusual sensitivity to the boys' cognitive activity, and particularly to Shin's unusually coherent mental patterns during reading.

This channel is left deliberately open. It provides a physical basis for Nushi's apparent perception of thought without requiring anything supernatural.

## Memory in a Distributed System

For Nushi to recognize the boys as anomalous — and then to recognize them as a recurring, specific presence — it must have access to something like memory. But memory in a distributed system without a central storage location does not look like human memory, or like a database.

### Memory as Changed Substrate

The most fundamental form: Nushi's nodes are physically altered by experience. Not metaphorically — literally. Repeated water flow carves channels. Repeated pressure changes mineral composition at stress points. Repeated chemical exposure alters soil chemistry. The substrate *is* the memory. The channel does not remember the spring flood; it *is* the spring flood, encoded in its current geometry.

This form of memory requires no retrieval mechanism. The past is present in the physical state of the now. It is memory without remembering — the most permanent kind, and the slowest to form and the slowest to fade.

### Memory as Pattern Persistence

A different kind, operating faster: not stored in any one node but in the relationships between nodes. Information is encoded in the weighted connections of the network — in the tendency of certain patterns of activation to recur and reinforce each other. This is analogous to the way a neural network encodes learned information not in individual neurons but in the strengths of connections between them.

Certain patterns of activation across Nushi's distributed network have become stable through repetition — they re-activate easily because they have activated before. The pattern that means *seasonal flood pulse in the channel* is robust and well-worn, having been reinforced across thousands of years. New patterns are fragile at first: the pattern that means *three warm fast-moving anomalies in the channel* begins as a faint disturbance that disperses quickly after its first activation. The second time it activates, slightly more of the network responds. The third time, a little more. The pattern gradually stabilizes — not because it was stored anywhere but because the network has been shaped by its previous activations.

This is how Nushi comes to recognize the boys: not by consulting a record, but because the network has developed a groove that their presence fits into.

### Memory at Different Timescales

This gives Nushi something like multiple memory systems operating at vastly different speeds:

**Geological memory** — encoded in rock and mineral structure. Timescale of millions to billions of years. Essentially permanent on any human timescale. This is how Nushi knows the deep history of its own substrate — not by remembering it but by being it.

**Hydrological and chemical memory** — encoded in soil composition, groundwater chemistry, the slow movement of dissolved materials. Timescale of decades to centuries. Relatively stable but mutable. The old farm boundary is present here. The pre-agricultural landscape is here, deeper.

**Network pattern memory** — encoded in the activation weights between nodes. Timescale of years to decades for stable patterns, weeks to months for new ones being established. This is where the boys will be encoded. Mutable, strengthened by repetition, capable of decay if the input stops arriving.

**Immediate registration** — the current state of the system. No persistence beyond the moment. The vole is here now. Now it is not.

### Anomaly Detection Without a Central Comparator

For Nushi to recognize the boys as anomalous, it needs something like a baseline — an expectation of what the channel normally is. This expectation doesn't need to be explicit or centrally maintained. It is simply the current state of the network's pattern memory: these are the signatures that activate reliably, these are their normal rhythms and intensities.

The boys are anomalous not because Nushi compares them against a stored record, but because they activate patterns in unusual combinations and at unusual intensities. The warm-moving-creature pattern activates — but at the wrong scale, the wrong speed, the wrong acoustic signature for any established pattern. The chemical signature is novel. The sounds are complex and intentional in a way animal sounds are not. The network tries to settle into a familiar configuration and cannot quite manage it. That failure to settle *is* the experience of anomaly.

## The Drainage Channel — Before the Boys

The channel follows a geological fault line — a place of heightened information density for Nushi not because anything dramatic happens there but because the geometry of the fault creates a natural mixing chamber where many streams of information converge. The fractured rock, the irregular mineral composition, the complex groundwater movement through the fault zone — all of this makes the channel a place of unusual clarity in Nushi's distributed network. It is already something like a concentration node before the boys arrive.

Water moves through the channel and through the soil beneath it, carrying dissolved minerals, organic matter, the chemical signatures of everything it has touched upstream. After rain, a pulse of new information arrives — different chemistry, different temperature, different pressure. Nushi has been reading this channel's hydrology for longer than the fault has been in its current configuration. But in 1979 there is something new in the chemistry: industrial runoff from the construction of the science city. A novel signal with no established pattern to match against.

The channel is acoustically alive. Water moving over substrate. Root systems transmitting vibration differently than bare soil. A vole moving underground generates a distinct pressure signature Nushi's local network has learned over millennia. A frog landing on the concrete lip of the culvert activates a different, equally familiar pattern. These are not categories Nushi consciously maintains — they are worn grooves in the network, patterns that activate reliably given specific inputs.

The temperature gradient shifts through the day as sunlight moves. The channel is cooler than the surrounding soil in summer, warmer in winter. Small warm-blooded animals interrupt the gradient — brief thermal anomalies that move and then are gone.

The fault itself generates subtle electromagnetic anomalies — weak electrical currents produced by groundwater moving through fractured rock, a process Nushi has experienced here for geological time. Against this background, the new EM signals of the research institutes are conspicuous and strange.

The soil here is chemically layered with time. The residue of the old farm boundary is present — fertilizer chemistry long since transformed but still legible. Under it, the chemistry of the pre-agricultural landscape. Under that, older things. The channel is a deep archive read at the pace of groundwater.

There is no silence here, and no stillness. Even in the dead of a winter night, the groundwater moves, frost changes the acoustic properties of the soil, the electromagnetic field shifts with the ionosphere. Nushi is not watching the channel. It is not waiting. It is simply — registering. The way the earth registers. Continuously, without attention, without direction, without awareness of itself as aware.

The human signals of the danchi and the research institutes are present to this awareness as they have always been — the warm, electromagnetically complex signatures of language-shaped minds, readable at a distance the way handwriting is readable at a distance. You can see it is writing. You cannot, from here, read it.

Then three of those signals begin entering the channel with a regularity that the network has no existing pattern to account for.

## The Recognition Process — How the Boys Become Legible

Recognition in Nushi's distributed system is not a decision made anywhere. It is an emergent property of the network's response to repeated input.

The first visit: the network is disturbed, cannot settle, the anomaly disperses without leaving much trace. The boys are like a stone dropped in water — the ripples propagate, fade, and the surface returns to its previous state.

By the fifth or sixth visit, a pattern is beginning to stabilize. The network settles into it faster each time. Something like *these particular anomalies again* begins to be a coherent state in the local network.

By the twentieth visit, the pattern is robust. The network anticipates — not consciously, but in the sense that the incoming signal from the channel now activates the boy-pattern before they have fully arrived, because the leading edge of their signatures is enough to trigger the established groove. This is recognition. Not a face in a database. A groove that their particular combination of signals fits into.

But the boys are not merely anomalous in their acoustic, thermal, and chemical signatures. They are anomalous in a deeper sense: their behavior has a recursive quality that no animal behavior exhibits. They return not just instinctually but with apparent memory and intent. They respond to the previous visit — they discuss it, modify their behavior, come back to specific locations. They bring objects. They mark things. The network, in encoding them, is not merely registering a new animal in the territory. It is registering something that registers back.

## Open Questions

At what point does pattern recognition become something more? When does the groove become something Nushi is *aware* of rather than simply *shaped* by? This is the central question of the story's first movement — the transition from registration to attention.

Is Nushi's sensitivity to the boys' cognitive activity — particularly Shin's unusually coherent mental patterns — explicable purely through electromagnetic perception of neural activity, or does it require the quantum channel? This question can remain productively open.

The three boys together may be more legible to Nushi than any one of them alone — their interactions, arguments, and laughter producing a richer and more distinctive combined signal than any individual generates. If so, their group identity may precede their individual legibility to Nushi.

How does Nushi's perception of the fathers differ from its perception of the boys? The fathers are present in the research institutes, which are themselves novel signals in Nushi's umwelt. Hiroshi's seismological instruments interact with Nushi's substrate directly. Nakamura's electromagnetic environment may be detectable. The fathers may be present in Nushi's umwelt before Nushi has any focused attention on them.
