# Tsukuba — Project Index

A story set in Tsukuba Science City, Japan, circa 1979-1980. Inspired by classic Japanese children's science fiction films (Gamera et al.) and Ray Bradbury's Dandelion Wine. Three boys living in a purpose-built research community encounter an ancient distributed intelligence that has inhabited the earth since its formation.

## Key Themes

- Ancient versus modern — Nushi predates life itself; the boys live at the frontier of human knowledge
- Observation versus connection — both Nushi and the boys begin as observers and become something more
- The permeability of childhood — children as naturally more receptive to what adults have learned to filter out
- Complementary ways of knowing — earth sciences and cognitive science, folklore and empirical research, ancient poetry and modern investigation
- The asymmetry of the relationship — and what genuine connection means across that asymmetry

## Key Details to Remember

- Taro's red baseball cap stirs without wind near Nushi's concentration nodes
- Shin's notebook will become important
- The drainage channel follows an ancient geological fault — Nushi's body
- The underground anomaly appears in Hiroshi's seismological data as an unexplained irregularity
- The Man'yoshu poem about souls meeting on Tsukuba in dreams connects to the hypnagogic contact
- Nushi's first deliberate act is small and almost tender — keeping the boys' territory dry
- Taro takes personal credit for choosing such a good place
