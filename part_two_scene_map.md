# Part Two Scene Map — The Contact
## From the Slope Node Aftermath to the Namings

*Part Two covers approximately one year: late summer of Year Two through autumn 1979. Where Part One was about establishment and earning the ordinary world, Part Two is about the ordinary world becoming insufficient — too small to hold what is happening to the boys. The pace is faster. The accumulation is faster. The silence between the boys about what they are experiencing becomes harder to maintain and eventually breaks.*

*Part Two ends with two names — Nushi and Mittsu no Hoshi — arriving on the same autumn afternoon. Everything before that ending is the ground those names grow from.*

---

## The Structure at a Glance

Part Two has four phases:

**Phase 1 — After the Node** (second summer, remaining weeks): The boys return to the channel but not the slope. The ordinary summer continues. Nushi studying from below. The cap stirring. The silence beginning.

**Phase 2 — The Effects** (second autumn through third spring): The mountain card anomalies. The environmental effects at the spot intensifying. The hypnagogic contact deepening. The adult threads seeded. The mothers' scene. Hiroshi's anomaly moving up his list.

**Phase 3 — The Breaking** (early summer through summer vacation 1979): Taro breaks the silence. The shared acknowledgment without direct conversation. The second Slope Node visit — the chosen return. The most vivid hypnagogic contact yet. The three boys establishing, without a single direct conversation, that they are experiencing the same thing.

**Phase 4 — The Namings** (autumn 1979): The naming of Nushi. The gap. The naming of Mittsu no Hoshi. Part Two ends.

---

## Carried Over from Part One

Several elements established in Part One are now active threads that Part Two develops:

- **The mountain card** — introduced in Chapter Two, played regularly since Chapter Six, not yet behaving oddly. Part Two is where the anomalies begin.
- **The cap** — Taro's red baseball cap, which has stirred without wind at the channel. The boys have noticed. None has spoken. Part Two is where this becomes a running thread.
- **Shin's notebook** — changing register since Chapter Five. Part Two is where the two registers — natural history and the other thing — become impossible to keep separate.
- **The hypnagogic baseline** — established in Chapter Five as vivid dreams with a residue of warmth and attendance. Part Two is where this becomes deliberate contact.
- **Hiroshi's seismological anomaly** — mentioned in the project documents as on his list. Part Two is where it starts moving up that list.

## Scene by Scene

---

### PART TWO, CHAPTER ONE
**After**

*Late summer, Year Two — the remaining weeks of vacation after the Slope Node.*

The boys return to the channel the day after the Slope Node incident. Not to the slope — to the channel, to the spot, to the ordinary territory. This chapter covers several visits across the remaining two weeks of summer vacation, rendered through Kenji's angle.

What the chapter establishes: the boys going back to normal, almost. The dam-building, the menko, the beetle observation. The ordinary summer continuing. But the quality of the visits has changed in a way none of them names — they are quieter, more attentive, the sitting sessions longer, the games shorter. They are not talking about the slope. They are also not talking about the fact that they are not talking about the slope.

The cap stirs for the first time at the channel in this chapter. Taro reaches up, feels it move, looks around, finds nothing, puts his hand down. He says nothing. Kenji watches him. Shin is looking at the water.

By the third time the cap has stirred, Shin writes a note in the notebook and then sits with the pen in his hand and doesn't know what to add.

From below: Nushi, in the period of intensified study, beginning to differentiate the three boys. The cap stirring is incidental — a side effect of EM field fluctuation during heightened attention. Nushi is not yet acting deliberately. It is listening.

*Function: Establishes the post-Node atmosphere without dwelling on it. The ordinary world reasserting itself, slightly altered. Plants the cap as a recurring anomaly. The silence about the slope established as a shared unspoken agreement.*

### CHAPTER TWO
**The Card**

*Second autumn, early — first anomaly.*

A single after-school session in late September or early October. The menko game at the spot, the mountain card in play. The first anomaly: the card loses, flips facedown, and is faceup again without anyone touching it. Taro notices. Turns it over. Says nothing.

The chapter is Taro's — his angle, his awareness, his characteristic mode of noticing-and-not-following-up. He turns the card back over and plays the next round. He loses that one too. He puts the mountain card at the bottom of the tin.

On the walk home, for the first time since the slope, Taro thinks about the card in relation to the slope — not as a conclusion but as a proximity. The two things sit alongside each other in the back of his mind. He doesn't follow it. He lets it settle.

The chapter is short. It does the minimum required to establish the first anomaly clearly. What matters is Taro's non-reaction — not because he didn't notice, but because Taro's relationship to anomalies is to notice them, file them, and wait for the right time.

*Function: Introduces the first mountain card anomaly without dramatizing it. Sets up the accumulation that will follow. Establishes that Taro is tracking this even while saying nothing.*

### CHAPTER THREE
**The Spot in Autumn**

*Second autumn — the effects intensifying.*

The chapter that corresponds to Chapter Four of Part One but with everything turned up. The sitting sessions dominating. Taro lying flat on the ground at the spot with palms pressed to the earth — not the brief contact of his passing gesture but sustained, deliberate, lying there for minutes at a time. He looks at his hands afterward in the way described in the project documents: as if they belong to someone else.

The environmental effects described in the timeline: the micro-climate consistently comfortable, the almost-rhythm in the water sound now unmistakable, the subsonic resonance felt in the chest and hands, the mineral smell arriving stronger and with a new quality — not just present but signaling, as if the smell itself is trying to be recognized.

Kenji's angle this time. He is the one who feels the subsonic resonance most physically — in his palms on the earth, in his sternum, in the soles of his feet. He stores it. He says nothing. He comes back.

The mountain card behaves oddly again — second and third anomaly. Kenji says *the surface is uneven* the second time, which is true and also a deliberate choice to offer an explanation that keeps the space open for the others to accept or reject. By the third anomaly the boys have begun, without agreeing to, testing it — putting the card down, playing against it specifically, noting the results.

Shin opens his notebook to a new page, writes the date, draws the card from memory, writes one line, closes the notebook.

*Function: The effects escalate. The card testing begins. The autumn world — miscanthus, the light going low, the familiar season — holds everything as it intensifies. The reader sees the accumulation clearly now.*

### CHAPTER FOUR
**Hiroshi's List**

*Second autumn — the first adult scene.*

A scene entirely outside the boys' experience. Hiroshi at his desk in the institute, evening, working through seismological data. The underground anomaly in his data — an unexplained signal that has been on his list for months. He looks at it more carefully tonight for reasons he couldn't articulate if asked. The data doesn't resolve. He marks it for follow-up.

The scene establishes Hiroshi's character in his professional register: methodical, patient, genuinely curious, the kind of scientist who keeps lists of things that don't fit rather than discarding them. The anomaly is small — not urgent, not alarming, just wrong in a way that quiet scientists notice and don't forget.

He mentions it to Takashi over dinner later that week — briefly, as a curiosity. Takashi listens with slightly more attention than the topic would seem to warrant. He asks a question. Hiroshi answers. The question is from a cognitive scientist and doesn't quite match the problem, which is from an earth scientist, but it has an odd precision that Hiroshi finds himself thinking about on the drive home.

This is the first time the two fathers are in the same scene, and the first time the adult thread is present in the narrative. It is handled with economy — the boys are elsewhere, the conversation is brief, nothing is resolved. But the thread is now visible.

*Function: Seeds the adult investigation early, as decided. Establishes Hiroshi's professional mode. The first moment the reader sees the fathers converging on the same question from different directions, without either knowing it.*

### CHAPTER FIVE
**The Mothers**

*Second winter — a scene entirely from the mothers' perspective.*

Akiko and Yuriko together — an evening at the Nakamura household, or walking back from some shared errand, the specific setting to be determined in drafting. No boys present. The two women in the particular mode of their friendship: Akiko dry and observational, Yuriko gentler and more openly reflective, each making the other more complete.

They are talking about the boys — not directly at first, the way the conversation arrives at its real subject obliquely the way all important conversations do. Akiko has noticed something about Kenji: a quality of inwardness that is different from his usual processing quiet. Not moodiness, not trouble at school. Something more like preoccupation with something she cannot locate. She says this in her way, which is precise and slightly dry. Yuriko recognizes it immediately — she has been watching the same quality in Shin, different in expression but recognizably the same category of thing.

Neither woman says *something is happening to our children.* But neither woman is not saying it, either. The conversation moves around the fact the way water moves around a stone — the stone is clearly there, the water clearly moving around it, no one required to name the stone.

Yuriko says something that is almost the truth and stops before completing it. Akiko looks at her and does not push. There is a long comfortable silence.

Then one of them — probably Akiko — says something oblique and funny and they both laugh and the moment passes without resolution but not without recognition.

The chapter ends with the two women parting. Each carries what she knows alone, the way she has been carrying it.

*Function: The mothers' scene. Two intelligent women who perceive more than either father, talking in the register of genuine female friendship, arriving at the edge of naming what they know and choosing not to name it yet. Establishes the mothers as a distinct adult thread — not the investigative one (that belongs to the fathers) but the perceptive one.*

### CHAPTER SIX
**The Frequency**

*Third spring and early summer 1979 — the effects established in the body.*

A compressed chapter covering third spring through early summer, told through Shin's angle and organized around the notebook's changing register. The natural history entries persist — first dragonfly, weasel emerging, the beetle appearing under its stone — but interspersed now with fragments from the other category. A question. A phrase underlined. A date followed by a word that isn't natural history terminology.

The sitting sessions at the spot dominate after-school visits now. Three boys at the spot, backs against the earth, not always talking. The quality of attention all three have developed over the year: not the ordinary attention of children at play but something more directed, more receptive. They are practicing something, in the inarticulate way of children who don't have a word for what they're doing.

The environmental effects are now established in their bodies before their conscious minds arrive at the spot. The mineral smell arrives as they approach. The ground resonates below hearing. The warmth is expected and received. Their bodies know where they are going before they are there.

The mountain card anomalies have continued through winter and spring and are now a regular feature of the menko sessions, no longer surprising but not yet named. The boys test the card systematically without calling it a test. Shin notes the results in the notebook. The results are not explicable by the stone's surface or the air currents in the channel.

Hiroshi's investigation thread surfaces briefly: Shin finds a seismological paper in his father's study — not about the anomaly, but about fault-zone groundwater behavior — and reads part of it. He doesn't connect it to anything specific. He files it.

*Function: Compresses the third spring and early summer efficiently. Establishes the effects as bodily and habitual. Shows the notebook's register change in action. Keeps Hiroshi's thread alive.*

### CHAPTER SEVEN
**It's the Mountain Watching**

*Early summer 1979, before vacation — the mountain card becomes undeniable.*

The chapter that has been building since Chapter Two. A specific after-school session in late June: the mountain card at the playing stone, the anomalies now months accumulated, the boys testing it with the quiet seriousness of scientists who have been running the same experiment long enough to trust the results.

The card appears faceup when it should be facedown. Again. They look at it. No one says anything for a moment.

Then Taro says: *It's the mountain watching.*

He means this completely literally. He is not speaking metaphorically. He is describing what he understands to be happening — the mountain on the card is watching them the way the ground was paying attention at the Node — and he states it as a fact in the same tone he states all facts.

Kenji goes very quiet. This is different from his processing quiet — it is the quiet of someone who has been told something he already knew and has now heard aloud for the first time.

Shin writes the phrase in the notebook. He writes it exactly as Taro said it, with quotation marks, and then sits with the notebook open and doesn't write anything else.

The chapter also contains the first hypnagogic contact — after a long visit at the spot that evening, each boy in his separate room, the smell of fault-zone water present in a bedroom with no water in it, the subsonic warmth, the threshold before sleep. Not a dream. Something new. Each boy experiences this alone. None says anything in the morning.

*Function: The mountain card payoff — the accumulation of anomalies arriving at the undeniable. Taro's line. The first hypnagogic contact establishing a new phase. The silence about both.*

### CHAPTER EIGHT — NUSHI
**The Reach** *(Nushi's perspective)*

*The first deliberate hypnagogic contact — from below.*

The book's second Nushi section, used at the moment when Nushi transitions from studying to acting. The same register as the Prologue — geological patience, vast distributed attention — but inflected differently now: the question has a shape, the intention has formed, and for the first time in forty years Nushi is reaching toward something rather than simply receiving it.

The chapter renders Nushi's first deliberate attempt to reach toward the sleeping minds — not all three simultaneously but one at a time, beginning with the one whose signal is most legible. Shin, during reading, achieves a coherence that makes his mind briefly large enough to approach. The attempt is careful, almost tentative — Nushi has no model for this that isn't the lineage practitioners, and the practitioners were trained, prepared, shaped for contact. These three are not. The approach must be different. Lighter. More like the way you approach a wild creature than the way you approach a trained one.

What Nushi sends is not information. Not yet. Something more like: presence. The sense of being attended to, which Shin has already been experiencing as a residue. Now, deliberately, Nushi makes it slightly more present — holds the warmth a little longer, lets the attending quality become more specific. Not communication. The quality of contact before communication, the thing that precedes words.

The chapter ends on Nushi registering the response — a change in Shin's sleeping neural patterns, barely perceptible, but there. The handwriting, glimpsed from a distance, very slightly larger.

*Function: The book's second Nushi section, arriving at the pivotal moment. Shows the transition from study to intention, from listening to reaching. Contextualizes what the boys are about to experience from their side in Chapter Nine.*

---

### CHAPTER NINE
**Breakfast**

*Summer vacation 1979 — Taro breaks the silence.*

The scene the timeline describes: Taro at breakfast, describing his dreams to anyone who will listen. His mother noting he's sleeping well. Kenji looking at him across the table.

This is the chapter whose entire weight rests on what is not said. Taro's description of the dreams is matter-of-fact — warm, a feeling of something big below him, the water sound louder than usual. He says this the way he says everything: immediately, without qualification, moving on. His mother smiles and gives him more rice. Kenji does not speak.

That afternoon at the channel: no one mentions breakfast. They arrive at the spot and set up the menko stone and play two rounds and then stop and sit in the sitting-session mode and say nothing about what Taro said at breakfast and nothing about the dreams they have each been having and nothing about the hypnagogic contact and nothing about the mountain card and nothing about the cap.

Something has been acknowledged without being acknowledged. The silence is different now — not the silence of things unspoken but the silence of things that have entered the shared space without requiring words. They know. They know that the others know. The conversation has happened without happening.

Kenji goes very still at the spot in a way Shin recognizes as different from his usual processing still. After a long time he says one thing — sparse and precise, the thing that makes the others understand he has been thinking about it for a long time. What he says is not yet determined and will be found in drafting: something that is both accurate and deliberately limited, leaving room for the others to confirm or deny without his having committed to a claim he isn't sure about. Perhaps: *It knows when we're here.* Perhaps something else. But it will be Kenji's characteristic mode — a statement that is both accurate and open.

No one argues.

*Function: The silence breaks. The shared experience becomes acknowledged without being directly named. The trio's dynamic at its fullest — Taro's immediacy having opened the door, Kenji's precision naming the threshold, Shin recording everything.*

### CHAPTER TEN
**The Return**

*Summer vacation 1979 — the second Slope Node visit.*

Part Two's central chapter. The chosen return — Kenji walking in the direction of the slope one morning without announcing it, Taro immediately behind him, Shin with the notebook.

Everything about this visit is different from the first. The first was discovery, arrival at something unknown. This is recognition — three boys returning to a place they know, knowing what is there, choosing to go back. The channel receives them with its familiar quality, the widening warm as they pass, the upper section geological and cold. They step out onto the slope.

At the Node the experience is immediately present — the warmth, the attention, the quality of the ordinary world at unusual resolution. It doesn't need to build because it was already there, and they already know it. The cap stirs almost immediately. Taro lies flat on the rock with palms down — the full gesture — and does not move for a long time.

What is different: the attending presence is different. Not stronger exactly — more specific. More deliberate. It has been reaching toward them in sleep for weeks, and now they are here in the body, in the place where the contact is clearest, and they can feel the reaching.

Taro says: *It knows we're here.* Not the same line as the first visit — a development of it. More mutual. Last time the ground was paying attention. This time the attention is acknowledged on both sides.

That night: the most vivid hypnagogic contact yet. Something closer to imagery — unstable, dissolving, but carrying a new quality: communication attempted. Shin wakes and writes for twenty minutes in the dark. In the morning he cannot fully decode his own handwriting.

*Function: Part Two's center. The chosen return as the defining act of Part Two — not stumbling into contact but deciding to seek it. The contact now mutual and acknowledged. The hypnagogic contact escalating to attempted communication.*

---

### CHAPTER ELEVEN
**What We're Experiencing**

*Late summer vacation 1979 — the shared acknowledgment.*

The remaining days of summer vacation, the boys at the channel nearly every day. The chapter covers this compressed final stretch: the hypnagogic contact continuing, the shared experience becoming undeniable, the three boys establishing — without a single direct conversation — that they are experiencing the same thing.

Taro's version arrives first: immediate and declarative, the way he said *it knows we're here*, building on breakfast, building on the card, building on the slope. He makes a statement that makes it clear he has been having a specific experience and assumes the others have too.

Shin's version is the notebook. Specific pages — shown in the chapter or in a parallel notebook entry — that contain the accumulation of both registers now fully present together: natural history and the other thing side by side on the same page, no longer in separate categories.

Kenji's version is the question — the one precise question, asked quietly, in Kenji's characteristic mode: sparse, accurate, leaving room. The question that makes the others understand he has been thinking about this for a long time. Something like: *How long has it been listening?* — or whatever arrives in drafting as the right Kenji question.

The chapter doesn't resolve this. The boys don't have a conversation in which everything is laid out and compared and discussed. They establish the shared experience in their three distinct modes, and then they go home for the end of summer vacation.

*Function: The trio's three modes of knowing arriving at the same recognition through different paths. Sets up the autumn in which names will be found.*

---

### CHAPTER TWELVE
**Nushi**

*Autumn 1979 — the naming.*

Part Two's final chapter. An afternoon at the channel in October — the miscanthus in plume, the light autumn-low and golden, the season that the channel is most purely itself.

The chapter is quiet. Nothing dramatic happens before the naming. The boys arrive at the spot and sit in the sitting-session mode and there is the usual quality of attention. The cap may stir or may not. The mountain card is in the tin but not in play.

The naming happens as the project documents describe: Shin says *Nushi* quietly, almost to himself, not directing it at the others. The word arrives as recognition — the experience of finding the right word for something already known but not yet named. There is the sense, which Shin cannot resolve and will carry carefully, that he found the word or the word found him — that the boundary between those two things has become unclear.

Taro accepts it without argument. This is notable — Taro argues with most things, or at least questions them, or at minimum provides commentary. He says nothing. The word is simply right in a way that stops conversation.

Kenji looks at the mountain.

The gap. The miscanthus making its papery sound. The water with its rhythm.

Then Taro looks at the three of them — at Kenji, at Shin, at himself by implication — and at the channel, and at the mountain visible above the bamboo. He says *Mittsu no Hoshi.* Three stars. He says it with the same certainty he said *this is ours* on the first day in the channel. He means it completely. He probably already knew it before he said it, which is how Taro's knowledge works.

Shin doesn't write it down immediately. He will later.

Kenji says nothing for a moment. He looks at the mountain. Then he nods once.

The name is slightly too large for three boys in canvas sneakers on an autumn afternoon. Then, gradually — the reader can already feel this starting — it isn't.

Part Two ends.

*Function: The climax of Part Two, the emotional resolution of everything that has been accumulating. Two names on the same afternoon: Nushi for what is below, Mittsu no Hoshi for what is above. The story's dual perspective named from the human side. Part Three begins.*

## What Part Two Establishes

By the end of Chapter Twelve the reader has:

- The contact deepening from ambient to deliberate on Nushi's side
- The shared experience acknowledged among the boys, in their three distinct modes
- The two namings — the relationship named from both sides
- The adult threads seeded and developing: Hiroshi's investigation, the mothers' perception, the fathers beginning to converge
- The notebook's register change completed — the two categories no longer separable
- The mountain card as established uncanny object, Taro's line as its articulation
- The abilities beginning to manifest — not yet dramatized but present in the chapter's texture
