# Birthdays and Milestones — The Three Boys
## Specific Dates and the Ceremonial Calendar

*This document establishes exact birthdays, fixes the boys' ages at key story events, and maps the Japanese childhood ceremonies and milestones that mark their lives — past, present, and coming.*

---

## Birthdays — Established

### Kenji — 森健二
**Born: April 18, 1968**

An April birthday places him among the oldest in his school year — Japanese school years run April to March, and a child born in April enters the same cohort as children born the following January through March. Kenji at the start of the story's main year (April 1979) has just turned 11, fresh into 5th grade. He is at the older end of his class without this being a defining fact about him.

April 18 is after the school entrance ceremony (early April) but close enough to it that his birthday and the new school year arrive together as a single cluster of spring. His mother cuts his hair with kitchen scissors for the entrance ceremony photo each year. By 5th grade he has opinions about this.

The wristwatch from his father — slightly too adult for him — was a birthday gift. The specific birthday is not stated in any existing document; this establishes it.

### Shin — 中村真
**Born: October 14, 1969**

An October birthday means Shin is among the younger half of his school year. In 4th grade during the story's main year, he turns 10 in October 1979 — during the autumn channel visits of Chapter Four, while the miscanthus is in plume. He is 9 for the channel's first summer (Year One), and turns 10 in the autumn of that year. He is 10 for the second summer — the Slope Node summer.

October 14 is close to Shichi-Go-San season (November 15), which gives his mother an annual double occasion in autumn — birthday, then shrine visit in the years when those applied.

His birthday falls in the middle of the school term, unremarkably. He would not expect it to be remarkable. He notes it in his notebook with a single line.

### Taro — 森太郎
**Born: January 29, 1970**

A January birthday makes Taro the youngest in his school year by a significant margin — the cutoff for Japanese school enrollment is April 1, so a child born in January enrolls with children born the previous April through March. Taro at the start of the story's main year is still 8, turning 9 in January 1979, a few months before the channel summer. He is 8 for the first channel summer. He turns 9 in January of Year Two, and is 9 for the second channel summer and the Slope Node incident.

This means the age gap between Kenji and Taro is not two years as it might appear but closer to three years of actual development — Kenji is a full three years older in lived experience during the story's key events. The four-inch height difference Taro considers a personal injustice is partly explained by this. He is not wrong that it is unjust.

January 29 is deep winter, cold, close to the New Year holidays. Taro's birthday falls when the channel is in its minimal-visit season. His mother makes his favorite food. Kenji gives him something that was probably purchased the day before.

## The Ceremonial Calendar — Past Milestones

### Shichi-Go-San 七五三
*Observed November 15 (or nearest weekend). Shrine visit, formal attire, chitose-ame candy.*

All three boys have passed through the Shichi-Go-San ages (3, 5, and 7 for boys) before the story begins.

**Kenji's Shichi-Go-San years:**
- Age 3: November 1971. Mori family still in Tokyo. Hiroshi took the day off, which he rarely did. Akiko has the photograph.
- Age 5: November 1973. Hakamagi ceremony — first hakama. Kenji stood very still for the photograph and looked exactly like his father.
- Age 7: November 1975. The last one. He was beginning to be self-conscious about formal dress. Akiko noticed and said nothing.

**Shin's Shichi-Go-San years:**
- Age 3: November 1972. Still in Tokyo. Yuriko dressed him carefully. He was cooperative and slightly bewildered.
- Age 5: November 1974. Hakamagi. He asked the priest a question during the ceremony. The priest answered it. Takashi has not forgotten this.
- Age 7: November 1976. By this age Shin was old enough to understand the ceremony's significance and approached it with the seriousness he brought to everything he considered worth understanding. He received his chitose-ame and held it as if it were a specimen.

**Taro's Shichi-Go-San years:**
- Age 3: November 1973. Coincides with Kenji's age-5 ceremony — both boys dressed formally at the same shrine visit. The family photograph from this day has them side by side: Kenji correct and still, Taro at a slight angle to the vertical, grinning. The scar through Taro's left eyebrow does not yet exist.
- Age 5: November 1975. Coincides with Kenji's age-7 ceremony. Taro's hakamagi. He was considerably more interested in the hakama as a garment than in its ceremonial significance. He asked whether he could keep wearing it. The answer was no. The scar through his left eyebrow now exists; it is visible in the photograph if you know where to look.
- Age 7: November 1977. The families have been in Tsukuba approximately one year. Taro goes to the local shrine with his parents. He is cooperative. He has questions about the chitose-ame that are addressed at some length.

*Note on the scar: it appears between the age-3 and age-5 Shichi-Go-San photographs. The incident occurred when Taro was 4, i.e. 1974. The family does not discuss it without sighing. Kenji was present. Taro does not hold this against him.*

### School Entrance Ceremonies 入学式
*Held early April. Children in formal wear carry their new randoseru under the cherry blossoms.*

**Kenji's entrance ceremony:** April 1975, age 6 (turning 7 that same month). He entered Tsukuba elementary school two years after the family arrived. The randoseru was purchased the previous month — black, as was standard for boys in 1975. He has been through five entrance ceremonies now (one for each grade change) and is entirely accustomed to them. His 5th grade entrance in April 1979 is the last before the story begins.

**Shin's entrance ceremony:** April 1976, age 6. He arrived in Tsukuba in 1977, which means he transferred mid-elementary rather than entering at the beginning — his entrance ceremony was in Tokyo, and his transfer to the Tsukuba school happened partway through 1st or 2nd grade. This is worth knowing: Shin is not a founding member of this school community the way local children are. He arrived and inserted himself efficiently and has been here long enough that it no longer shows, but he knows which friends predate him.

**Taro's entrance ceremony:** April 1977, age 7 (birthday is January, so he was already 7). This is his first ceremony in Tsukuba — the family arrived in time for him to begin 1st grade here. His randoseru is, by 1979, the one that is slightly too large for him, giving the impression of a backpack wearing a small boy. It contains, in its front pocket, a bent-corner tobacco tin.

## Upcoming Milestones — The Horizon

### What Is Coming for Kenji
Kenji is in 5th grade in the story's main year (1979). He will complete 6th grade in March 1981 and enter junior high school (*chūgakkō*) in April 1981, at age 12–13.

This is the most significant horizon in the story's timeframe. Junior high means a new school building, new teachers, new classmates drawn from multiple elementary schools, and — most importantly for the story — a new social world that Kenji will enter a full year ahead of Shin. The trio as currently constituted cannot survive this unchanged.

Kenji is not yet aware of this in any conscious way. He is 11 and at peak childhood and has no sense of its ending. But the story takes place in the last years before the transition. The channel, the spot, the three of them — all of it is happening in the borrowed time of late elementary school.

**The 6th grade field trip (*shūgaku ryokō*):** This overnight trip — typically two to three days — is a major event exclusive to 6th graders. Kenji will take this trip in autumn 1980, in the story's third year. He will be away from Tsukuba for several days. It is the first significant event in the story that separates him from the others.

### What Is Coming for Shin
Shin enters 5th grade in April 1980, one year behind Kenji. His junior high transition will come in April 1982. In the story's timeframe he follows Kenji's path with a year's delay — which means he will have one year in elementary school without Kenji there, a circumstance worth attending to.

**Upcoming birthday:** Shin turns 10 in October 1979 — during the autumn channel visits, while the miscanthus is in plume. He will note the date in his notebook. Nothing else is recorded about how he marks it, but his mother will make his favorite food and his father will give him a book.

### What Is Coming for Taro
Taro enters 3rd grade in April 1979. He has three more years of elementary school after the story's main year — his junior high transition is April 1983, well beyond the story's timeframe. In the immediate term, the most significant milestone is simply keeping up with Kenji and Shin, which he will continue to accomplish through sheer refusal to be left behind.

**Upcoming birthday:** Taro turns 10 in January 1980, mid-story, deep winter, when channel visits are minimal. He will be very clear about what he wants for his birthday. Some of it will arrive.

## Notes for Scenes

- **The Shichi-Go-San photograph** in which both Taro (age 3) and Kenji (age 5) appear together is a specific family object. It sits somewhere in the Mori household. Akiko knows where. Kenji does not think about it. Taro has seen it and finds it evidence that the hakama suited him better than anyone acknowledged.

- **Shin's transfer** from a Tokyo school to the Tsukuba school is a minor biographical fact with potential usefulness: he arrived not as a founding member but as someone who inserted himself into an established community. He did this competently and quietly. There may be a classmate who remembers that he was not always there.

- **The age gap between Kenji and Taro** is effectively three years of development, not two. In the channel this mostly doesn't show — the spot equalizes them. In other contexts it occasionally surfaces, and Taro is alert to any suggestion that it limits him.

- **The wristwatch** Kenji received on his birthday (April 18 — the most recent birthday before the story's first summer) is slightly too adult for him. He checks it when uncertain, as a grounding gesture rather than to know the time. It connects him to his father.
