# Mori Hiroshi — 森博

## Basic Information

- **Age:** Early-to-mid 40s
- **Role:** Father of Kenji and Taro
- **Field:** Earth sciences, seismology
- **Institution:** Tsukuba Science City research community
- **Family:** Mori family

## Physical Description

- **Build:** Lean and slightly weathered for an academic — insists on fieldwork when possible
- **Expression:** Quiet and inward-looking, thinking rather than withdrawing
- **Distinguishing Features:** Carries the physical evidence of a man who spends time outdoors despite being an academic

## Personality

- Profound quiet that his son Kenji inherited
- Mind always partly elsewhere — not from lack of love but genuine absorption in work
- Communicates with Kenji mostly through shared silence and occasional intense conversation
- Slightly bewildered by Taro but entirely charmed
- Found in earth science a way to rebuild a worldview shattered by postwar Japan — needs to understand foundations

## Relationships

- **Akiko:** Wife. She provides the organizational intelligence he lacks domestically. Deeply complementary.
- **Kenji:** Kindred spirit. They understand each other without many words.
- **Taro:** Loves him with a kind of delighted helplessness.
- **Nakamura Takashi:** Friend of decades, met at Todai during graduate studies in the late 1950s. Lost touch during busy early career years, reunited at Tsukuba.

## Background

- Born approximately 1937
- Grew up in postwar Japan, shaped by its instability both literal and metaphorical
- Attended Tokyo University (Todai), graduate studies late 1950s to early 1960s
- Connected with Nakamura Takashi through an informal cross-departmental study group
- Recruited to Tsukuba Science City, arrived within a year of the Nakamura family

## Nushi Connection

- His seismological equipment occasionally registers the underground anomaly node as something unexplained — on his list of things to investigate
- He is literally studying the medium in which Nushi exists without knowing it
- Core samples, seismic readings, geological surveys are all intimate to Nushi in ways Hiroshi cannot imagine
- May arrive at the truth of Nushi from the opposite direction his son approaches it — through instruments rather than dreams
- Might find the word Nushi quietly satisfying when he eventually hears it — a man who studies the earth has perhaps always half-suspected it has a presence

## Notes

- His field and Nakamura Takashi's field together cover almost the complete scope of what Nushi would want to understand about human civilization
- Whether the two families ending up at Tsukuba together is coincidental is an open story question
