# Timeline — Tsukuba
## From the Families' Arrival to First Conscious Contact

*Events are sequenced where sequence is known. Approximate calendar dates are given where they can be inferred. Items marked [UNDECIDED] flag open questions that need resolution.*

*A note on the school year: Japanese elementary school runs April to March, with three terms separated by short breaks. Summer vacation is approximately late July to late August — about five weeks, not three months. After-school time on ordinary school days is real and unsupervised for boys of this age — channel visits of an hour or less are entirely possible on weekdays throughout the year, constrained mainly by daylight and weather rather than by the school calendar. The channel is not a summer-only place. It is a summer-primary place that maintains a lower-intensity presence year-round.*

## Background — Before the Story Begins

### Late 1950s — Early 1960s
- Hiroshi Mori and Takashi Nakamura are graduate students at Tokyo University (Todai), connected through an informal cross-departmental study group — earth sciences and cognitive science in productive conversation
- They become friends of genuine depth, complementary in temperament and field
- Both begin academic careers; contact diminishes during the busy early years of research and family

### Mid-to-late 1960s
- Tsukuba Science City development begins in earnest; agricultural land is graded, paddy fields drained, the old farm drainage infrastructure partially replaced with concrete channels
- The drainage channel survives the development, caught between two institute property lines, administratively forgotten
- The last fully initiated practitioner of the Tsukuba shrine's kannagi lineage dies sometime in this decade [UNDECIDED — exact timing]; Nushi's channel of sustained human contact goes quiet for the first time in centuries

### Approximately 1968

### Approximately 1969

### Approximately 1970
- Taro born (age 9 in 1979)

## The Families Arrive in Tsukuba
### [UNDECIDED — exact year, probably 1977 or early 1978]

**The Nakamura family arrives first** — Takashi recruited to the cognitive sciences institute, Yuriko and Shin relocating from Tokyo. Yuriko begins the quiet work of making Tsukuba a home. Shin, seven or eight years old, begins at the local school. He has already read more than most adults.

**The Mori family arrives within a year** — Hiroshi recruited to the earth sciences institute, possibly through the same institutional process that brought Nakamura, possibly influenced by their prior connection [UNDECIDED — coincidence or not]. Akiko assesses the teachers, finds the grocery stores, introduces herself to the neighbors. Kenji and Taro enroll in school.

**The families reconnect.** Hiroshi and Takashi resume their friendship with the ease of genuine intellectual affinity. Akiko and Yuriko form their own friendship rapidly. The boys are introduced.

**Kenji and Shin's friendship begins** — inevitable rather than accidental. The one-year age difference is irrelevant. Taro comes with Kenji, as Taro always does.

## Year One — Discovery

### First Summer (late July — late August)

**The boys begin ranging freely through the danchi complex.** The science city is still half-built — construction sites, unfinished roads, gaps in fences. For three boys it is an extraordinary landscape.

**Discovery of the drainage channel.** The gap in the fence, the concrete culvert, the transition to the earthen upper section, the widening with its dry floor and bamboo shade. The sequence takes multiple exploratory sessions across the five weeks.

**The spot is claimed.** Taro declares it excellent territory. Kenji agrees without saying so. Shin writes the location in his notebook with the date and a sketch of the bamboo profile from the channel floor. The notebook is, so far, entirely natural history.

**Menko at the spot begins.** Taro's tin of cards comes to the channel. The flat playing stone is established.

**First beetle-hunting expeditions** — evening forays onto the lower slope above the channel, flashlights, the kunugi oaks. Taro's rhinoceros beetle takes up residence on the bedroom windowsill.

*From Nushi's perspective:* The channel has been quiet since the last practitioner's death. New animal signatures in a familiar corridor — present, noted, not yet requiring attention. But these creatures return with intention. They communicate. They go further each time. The itch of attention begins.*

### First Autumn Term (September — December)

**After-school visits establish the year-round pattern.** Two or three times a week, an hour rather than an afternoon. The spot is theirs in autumn too. The miscanthus produces its silver plumes. Taro uses them as improvised weapons. Shin presses one into the notebook.

**Daylight shortens but visits continue.** By November the boys navigate the upper section in low late-afternoon light with the confidence of children who know a place well. By December visits are limited to weekends.

**Nushi registers the year-round quality of their presence.** Not a summer event. A sustained relationship with the corridor that distinguishes these creatures from any prior animal use of it.

**The notebook accumulates autumn natural history** — last dragonfly sighting, date the frogs go quiet, weasel tracks in October mud. The unidentified ground beetle appears for the first time: drawn once, question mark beside it.

### First Winter (January — March)

**Channel visits become occasional Saturday checks** — cold, brief, purposeful. The spot is still dry. Still theirs.

**The hypnagogic baseline exists without the boys knowing it.** Nushi has been studying their sleeping minds since summer — listening at the threshold, not yet acting. Long winter nights, still bodies. The boys sleep well. Their dreams are vivid in ways they do not think to mention.

## Year Two — The Slope Node

### Second Spring Term (April — early July)

**The channel comes back to life.** After-school visits resume with renewed energy. The frogs return. The weasel kits emerge. The spot is as good as they left it.

*From Nushi's perspective:* The boys' return after the winter gap produces something in the network that functions like anticipation fulfilled. Their signatures activate the concentration zone before they have fully arrived.*

**Nushi's first deliberate act.** The drainage channel becomes subtly more hospitable — the spot stays dry after rain, the ground firmer than it should be, the overgrowth not encroaching. The change is gradual; there is no single moment when it begins. [NOTE: Placed here in the second spring, after the period of distant study. Could be earlier — UNDECIDED.]

**The boys notice the channel is unreasonably pleasant.** Taro takes personal credit for having chosen such a good place. Kenji accepts this without comment. Shin writes a note in the notebook and isn't sure why he wrote it. This is the first entry that is not natural history.

**The mountain card enters the story.** Taro's tin includes a card from a secondhand shop — a mountain in an older graphic style, slightly incongruous among the anime characters [UNDECIDED — exact occasion of acquisition]. Shin identifies it as probably Tsukuba from the south. It sits in the tin, not yet significant.

### Second Summer (late July — late August)

**The expedition along the channel toward the mountain.** The boys have been going a little further each summer. This time they follow the channel all the way to the lower slopes. Kenji first. Shin following. Taro refusing to be left.

**The Slope Node incident.** They find the place on the lower slope without knowing what it is — where Nushi's cognition achieves unusual clarity, where farmers have always felt uneasy and children dare each other. They spend an afternoon there during a period of Nushi's heightened cognitive activity.

The air feels strange. Taro's cap stirs without wind. They feel simultaneously that they should leave and that they absolutely don't want to. They stay longer than they intended. They go home in unusual quiet.

*That night: the first vivid dreams. Each boy alone. None mentions it.*

*From Nushi's perspective:* Three young humans in its most active concentration zone during heightened cognition produce neural resonance — something like a tuning fork responding to its matching frequency. Unprecedented. Nushi is, in whatever sense applies, startled.*

**The period of distant study intensifies.** The boys return to the channel but not immediately to the Slope Node. The ordinary summer continues. Nushi studies their sleeping minds with new focus and begins to differentiate the three: Kenji's intentional courage, Shin's neural coherence during reading, Taro's fractal patterning.

**The cap stirring becomes a recurring event at the channel.** Nushi modulating the EM field. The cap stirs when its attention is most directed. All three boys have noticed. None says anything.

*Shin writes a note after the third time. By the fifth time he has stopped writing about it because he doesn't know what to write.*

**Nushi discovers the fathers** — through the boys, registers their fields and their prior connection. Even a four-billion-year-old intelligence finds this noteworthy.

### Second Autumn Term (September — December)

**After-school visits continue, now quieter.** Less menko, less dam-building. More sitting at the spot. Even Taro is quieter — his quiet taking the form of lying flat on the ground with palms pressed to the earth rather than sitting still.

**The environmental effects begin in earnest:**
- The micro-climate at the spot — consistently comfortable
- The almost-rhythm in the water sound
- The subsonic resonance felt in the chest and hands
- The mineral smell of the spring water beginning to signal *something is about to be different*

**Taro puts his palms flat on the earth.** Without deciding to. He looks at his hands afterward as if they belong to someone else. He does this again the next visit, and the next. He says nothing about it.

**The menko mountain card begins to behave oddly.** First anomaly in an autumn session: faceup when it should be facedown. Taro notices. Turns it over. Says nothing. Second anomaly: *it did it again.* Kenji: *the surface is uneven.* Third anomaly and after: the boys begin, without agreeing to, testing it.

*Shin opens his notebook to a new page. Writes the date. Draws the card from memory. Writes one line. Closes the notebook.*

### Second Winter (January — March)

**Occasional Saturday visits.** Cold. Brief. The spot is still dry. The water still flows. The bamboo holds its color.

**The hypnagogic baseline deepens.** Long nights, quiet houses. Nushi is learning the specific quality of each boy's sleeping consciousness with increasing precision.

**Shin reads everything available about Tsukuba.** Man'yoshu references, shrine history, a folklore study from his father's study. He copies two poems into the notebook. He adds a note about a spring in the Hitachi Fudoki. The notebook is becoming something other than natural history, but he hasn't fully noticed yet.

**The unidentified ground beetle is drawn a second time, from a different angle.** Still no identification.

## Year Three — The Story's Main Year
### 1979

### Spring Term (April — early July)

**The channel in its third spring.** The boys move through it with complete familiarity. The spot is theirs without question.

**The environmental effects are now established in the boys' bodies** — if not yet their conscious minds. The spot is warm when it shouldn't be. The ground resonates below hearing. The mineral smell arrives before they reach the spot. Their bodies know where they are going before they are fully there.

**The sitting sessions dominate after-school visits.** Three boys at the spot, backs against the earth, not always talking. A new quality of attention in the time they spend there.

**Shin's notebook entries change register.** The careful natural history persists — first dragonfly, weasel emerging — but interspersed now with fragments from a different category. A question. A phrase. A date and a word underlined. The two registers exist in the same notebook without Shin having decided they belong to different categories.

### Early Summer — Before Vacation (June — late July)

**The mountain card anomalies become undeniable.** Months of informal testing have accumulated. The card consistently appears faceup when it should not. At the spot, on the playing stone.

**Taro says:** ***it's the mountain watching.*** He means this literally. Kenji goes very quiet. Shin writes the phrase in the notebook.

**The first hypnagogic contact.** After a long after-school visit in late June — the smell of fault-zone water present in a bedroom with no water in it. The subsonic warmth. The threshold before sleep. Not a dream. Something new.

**Each boy experiences this alone and says nothing.** The silence holds through the first weeks of summer.

### Summer Vacation (late July — late August, 1979)

**Five weeks. The boys at the channel nearly every day.**

**Taro breaks the silence** — at breakfast, describing his dreams to anyone who will listen. His mother notes he's sleeping well. Kenji looks at him across the table.

That afternoon at the channel: no one mentions breakfast. But something has been acknowledged without being acknowledged. Kenji goes very still. Shin opens his notebook.

**The second visit to the Slope Node.** Chosen, not accidental. Kenji suggests it by walking in that direction. Taro immediately agrees. Shin brings the notebook.

The second visit is different. They know something is here. The cap stirs almost immediately. They sit down. Taro lies flat with his palms to the earth and says: *it knows we're here.*

No one argues.

**That night: the most vivid hypnagogic contact yet.** Something closer to imagery — unstable, dissolving, but carrying a new quality: communication attempted, not merely presence felt.

Shin wakes and writes for twenty minutes in the dark. In the morning he cannot fully decode his own handwriting.

**Over the remaining days of summer vacation, the shared experience becomes undeniable.** The three boys establish — without a single direct conversation — that they are experiencing the same thing.

Taro's version: immediate and declarative.
Shin's version: the notebook.
Kenji's version: one precise question, asked quietly, that makes the others understand he has been thinking about this for a long time.

### Autumn Term (September — December, 1979)

**The naming.** On a specific afternoon at the channel — miscanthus in plume, light autumn-low and golden — Shin says *Nushi* quietly, almost to himself. It is simply right. Taro accepts it without argument. Kenji looks at the mountain.

**The naming of Mittsu no Hoshi** [UNDECIDED — same occasion or separate]. Shin suggests it quietly. Taro immediately and loudly approves. Kenji says nothing for a moment, looks at the mountain, nods once.

**The abilities begin to manifest:**
- Kenji: directional intuition, a fraction ahead of conscious decision
- Shin: occasional whole comprehension, dissolving before fully examined
- Taro: pattern completion — the world cooperating with him more than usual

**After-school visits take on new purpose.** The boys are practicing something, in the inarticulate way of children who don't have a word for what they're doing. Sitting at the spot with a specific quality of attention that all three recognize and none can describe.

**The adults begin to notice.** Nakamura Takashi observes Shin with professional attention and is uncomfortable with himself for doing it. Akiko watches Kenji and notes a change she cannot name. Yuriko recognizes unusual development with her teacher's instinct.

**Hiroshi's seismological anomaly moves up his list.** He mentions it to Takashi over dinner. Takashi listens with unusual attention and asks the questions of a cognitive scientist encountering an anomaly — which are the wrong questions, and somehow the right ones.

### Winter 1979—1980

**Channel visits minimal — cold, dark, occasional Saturday checks.**

**The hypnagogic contact is at its deepest.** Long winter nights, still houses. The imagery is becoming more stable, more coherent. Less dissolving. The communication is approaching something that might, eventually, be called mutual.

**Shin's notebook is filling.** The winter entries are different from anything before — longer, more associative, some written in the dark. Hiragana more frequent, as if the kanji he usually reaches for are not adequate. The word 主 appears several times, alone on a line.

**The unidentified ground beetle is drawn a third time, from a third angle.** Still no identification. The question mark has become three question marks.

**Hiroshi's investigation of the underground anomaly begins in earnest.** Winter fieldwork, core samples, a review of years of data. What he finds is not immediately explicable.

He tells Takashi more than he intended to over an evening of sake. Takashi's response is not what Hiroshi expected — he asks the wrong questions, or rather, the right questions from entirely the wrong direction.

**The two fathers are converging on the same question from opposite sides.** Instruments and data on one hand, observation and inference on the other. Their friendship, already deep, is about to become something more complicated and more important.

- Summer vacation: daily or near-daily, full afternoons — primary contact season
