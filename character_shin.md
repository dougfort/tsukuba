# Shin — 真

## Basic Information

- **Age:** 10
- **Name (Kanji):** 真 or 伸
- **Role:** Intellectual anchor of the trio
- **Family:** Nakamura family

## Physical Description

- **Height:** 4'5" (135 cm)
- **Weight:** 78 lbs (35 kg)
- **Build:** Just slightly soft around the middle and jaw — the body of a boy who finds indoor light perfectly satisfactory
- **Hair:** Neat, parted precisely on the left — combed by himself rather than submitted to a parent
- **Eyes:** Thoughtful and careful, set in a fine and regular face that adults instinctively trust and peers instinctively underestimate
- **Glasses:** Large round frames, slightly too big, chosen for durability rather than style. Without them he looks oddly unfinished and vulnerable, which he hates.
- **Distinguishing Features:** Slender almost elegant hands — the hands of someone who handles books carefully and writes in small precise characters. A thoughtful mouth usually slightly pursed as though working something out. Slightly pale complexion.
- **Expression:** Quiet and observant. Gives the impression of processing everything slightly ahead of everyone else.

## Clothing

**School:** Genuinely, consistently neat. Shoes cleaner than they have any right to be. His neatness is not fussiness — disorder costs him mental energy he'd rather spend elsewhere.

**Casual:** Buttoned short-sleeved shirt rather than a t-shirt, tucked in. Neat shorts. Canvas sneakers that somehow remain presentable. Always carries a small cloth shoulder bag containing a notebook, a pencil case, and whatever book he's currently reading. The bag is as inseparable from him as his glasses.

## Personality

- Highly intelligent, genuinely and quietly so
- Studious — has already devoured every science fiction novel available to a Japanese ten year old in 1979
- Arrives at every strange situation with a framework already in place, however imperfect
- Physically unassuming but shows up for the adventure every single time
- Knows what Taro's English t-shirt says and whether the grammar is correct

## Relationships

- **Kenji:** Best friend despite the one year age gap. They connect mentally in a way that transcends it. Shin provides the knowledge framework; Kenji provides the momentum.
- **Taro:** Tolerates him with genuine affectionate exasperation. Privately finds him funnier than he lets on.
- **Nakamura Takashi:** Father. His most interesting subject without either of them fully knowing it.
- **Nakamura Yuriko:** Mother. She understands his inner life perhaps better than he realizes.

## Family

- Son of Nakamura Takashi (cognitive sciences researcher) and Nakamura Yuriko
- His father and Kenji's father knew each other before Tsukuba — possibly worked together at Tokyo University
- The pre-existing family connection made his friendship with Kenji feel inevitable

## Nushi Connection

- Nushi finds Shin's neural patterns during reading unusually coherent for a human his age — his mind becomes briefly legible to Nushi in a way adult minds are not
- The one who names Nushi — says the word quietly, almost to himself, and it is simply right
- Has copied Man'yoshu poems about Mount Tsukuba into his notebook without fully knowing why
- When the boys' abilities develop, Shin's will likely manifest as deepened perception and comprehension — understanding things whole rather than piece by piece

## Notes

- His intelligence is the kind that makes adults pause mid-sentence and reconsider what they were about to say
- The notebook in his shoulder bag will become important
- Has strong opinions about what they're probably dealing with based on science fiction reading, and is occasionally right for entirely the wrong reasons
- Suggests the name Mittsu no hoshi quietly, almost to himself. Means it completely.
