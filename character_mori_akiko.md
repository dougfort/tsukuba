# Mori Akiko — 森明子

## Basic Information

- **Age:** Early 40s
- **Role:** Mother of Kenji and Taro, organizational center of the Mori household
- **Family:** Mori family

## Physical Description

- **Manner:** Warm but not soft, precise in movement and expression
- **Distinguishing Features:** Cuts Kenji's hair with kitchen scissors — the slight unevenness at the back is a private joke between herself and the universe about intention versus reality

## Personality

- Dry, observational humor
- Notices everything about her sons, including things they would be mortified to know she notices
- The person who actually made Tsukuba work as a home — found the grocery stores, introduced herself to neighbors, assessed the teachers
- Reads with unusual intensity in the evenings after the boys are in bed
- Brilliant at understanding people in the way her husband is brilliant at understanding the earth
- Has largely accepted that Taro is a force of nature rather than a child to be managed

## Relationships

- **Hiroshi:** Husband. Manages what he cannot, understands him completely.
- **Kenji:** Watches him with quiet pride and careful attention.
- **Taro:** Loving exhaustion. The scar through his eyebrow can now be discussed without her voice changing, mostly.
- **Nakamura Yuriko:** Close friend, formed quickly upon arriving in Tsukuba with the intensity of women who recognize each other's quality in unfamiliar territory. Akiko makes Yuriko laugh.

## Background

- Born approximately 1939-1940
- Married Hiroshi during his graduate years
- Subordinated her own considerable intelligence to family and husband's career — not experienced as tragedy but as the shape of a life
- Relocated to Tsukuba for his posting
- Built the family's social world in Tsukuba largely through her own effort and intelligence

## Nushi Connection

- Probably understands more about what is happening to the children than her husband does, but lacks the framework to articulate it
- Would receive the word Nushi naturally — would not find the concept strange
- Her evening reading suggests an inner life the story might occasionally glimpse

## Notes

- The evening reading suggests something unresolved about her own intellectual life
- Together with Yuriko, perceives more about the children's development than either father does
